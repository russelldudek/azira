My operating thesis

Build the decision nervous system. Do not become the company switchboard.

The highest-leverage Chief of Staff is not the person who personally knows everything. It is the person who builds mechanisms that help the right leaders see, decide, own, and learn.

Clarity is a system.

The role wins by making priorities legible, decisions explicit, commitments visible, risks early, and outcomes measurable.

My operating principle: influence through useful structure, not process for its own sake.

01 / PRIORITIES

Make tradeoffs explicit.

One visible set of company outcomes, strategic initiatives, owners, assumptions, resource tensions and confidence levels.

02 / DECISIONS

Reduce decision debt.

Clarify the decision, owner, evidence required, dissent, deadline and what changes after the answer.

03 / COMMITMENTS

Translate discussion into motion.

Every executive decision leaves the room with accountable owners, dates, dependencies and escalation triggers.

04 / VISIBILITY

Build one source of truth.

Revenue, profitability, customer health, product motion, strategic initiatives and risk in one executive view.

05 / LEARNING LOOP

Close the loop from outcome back to assumption.

QBRs should reveal which assumptions held, which failed, and what the company changes next—not merely narrate the quarter.

Signal-to-action architecture

Every important initiative should leave a visible trail.

A simple operating path prevents the two common extremes: endless discussion or premature execution.

Observe

See the signal.

Customer evidence, product data, financial performance, competitive movement, risk or operational friction.

Frame

Name the decision.

What exactly is being decided, by whom, by when, and what evidence would change the answer?

Commit

Translate into ownership.

Owner, milestones, dependencies, resourcing, tradeoffs, expected outcome and escalation conditions.

Measure

Watch leading signals.

Metrics connected to decision thresholds—not dashboards that glow red after the fact.

Learn

Update the system.

What assumption held, what failed, what changes next, and what becomes a durable mechanism?

Four operating loops

Where I would focus the work.

Each loop starts with a calibrated question because the wrong operating system can create more drag than the problem it was meant to solve.

“What is consuming CEO attention today that should become a repeatable company mechanism tomorrow?”

Increase CEO leverage by framing decisions, pressure-testing assumptions, sequencing priorities, preparing evidence and protecting attention—without isolating the CEO from operating reality.

Decision briefs

Priority architecture

Outcomes, strategic initiatives, dependencies, owner confidence and resource tension.

Board readiness

Strategy narrative, performance evidence, risk visibility, metric integrity and follow-through.

“Which executive meetings create decisions—and which merely create more meetings?”

Design leadership cadence around actual work: decision forum, operating review, QBR, annual planning and focused off-sites with explicit outputs.

Before

Pre-reads, explicit decisions, unresolved tensions, trusted metrics and clear asks.

During

Facilitation that keeps debate productive, captures decisions and makes dissent safe.

After

Owners, dates, dependencies, escalation conditions and visible closure.

“What would have to be visible for the ELT to know the company is off-plan two weeks earlier?”

Build an executive performance architecture connecting revenue, profitability, pipeline quality, customer health, delivery, product adoption, strategic initiatives and risk.

Lead indicators

Signals that reveal future pressure before lagging outcomes arrive.

Decision thresholds

Metrics linked to specific actions, owners and escalation paths.

Narrative discipline

What changed, why, what was learned and what leaders will do differently.

“Where is high-value work losing velocity at a functional boundary?”

Lead a small number of cross-functional missions where coordination failure materially slows revenue, customer value, product launches, profitability or strategic progress.

Mission charter

Outcome, owner, economics, decision rights, milestones, dependencies and stop conditions.

Mission room

Exit into system

Executive review question

What needs a decision—not another update?

  • Top decisions required this week
  • Cross-functional risks without a clear owner
  • Metrics outside agreed decision thresholds
  • Initiatives consuming attention without expected value
  • Customer or market signals challenging current assumptions
